# Real Estate Cost Segregation Timing: Why the Best Study Is Not Always the Latest One

**Originally published:** July 2024  
**Category:** Real Estate

## Summary

Cost segregation remains powerful, but the timing question is often more important than the study itself. Owners need to understand whether the property, holding horizon, and depreciation environment still support the desired result.

## Why timing matters

Acquisition, renovation, refinancing, and disposition planning all affect whether acceleration still creates the intended benefit. A study ordered too late or with the wrong objective can create work without delivering strategy.

## Practical review points

- current ownership horizon
- passive versus active income context
- state conformity and recapture implications
- coordination with financing and exit planning

## Bottom line

Cost segregation works best when it is part of the tax strategy, not just a standalone engineering project.
