# High-Income Audits Are Back: What the IRS's 2023 Enforcement Push Means for Affluent Filers

**Originally published:** September 2023  
**Category:** Individual

## Summary

The IRS's 2023 enforcement announcements made clear that high-income and complex returns are receiving renewed attention. That does not mean every affluent filer is headed for an audit, but it does mean documentation discipline matters more than it did during the agency's leaner years.

## What changed

The IRS publicly identified large partnerships and high-income taxpayers as enforcement priorities. That signaling matters because it shapes where examination resources are likely to be deployed.

## Returns most exposed

- returns with large pass-through losses
- complex partnership structures
- significant charitable or valuation-sensitive positions
- high-income filers with unresolved balances or incomplete records

## Practical response

Taxpayers should revisit return support, valuation files, charitable substantiation, and any position that depends heavily on narrative rather than documentary support.

## Bottom line

The best time to prepare for a more active audit environment is before a notice arrives, not after.

IRS enforcement announcements issued in September 2023 regarding high-income individuals and complex partnerships.
